Hello there! I’m Yomna, a Technology Fellow at the EFF (Electronic Frontier Foundation), and a software engineer at Stripe. I have a degree in mathematics from the University of Waterloo. At the EFF, I’ve worked on building tools to encrypt the web, fighting privacy-invasive ads, measuring the progress of AI research, and techniques to overcome cell network surveillance. At Stripe I work on our Queues team, which builds scalable infrastructure for handling streams of events in real time.

My research interests lie at the intersection of technology and society, especially (1) building technology informed by social justice to empower users, (2) using technology to create art that encourages empathy and curiosity. I’m especially passionate about both theoretical and applied cryptography, as well as art.
